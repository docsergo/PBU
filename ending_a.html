Ending A — Making Peace

The institution will outlast your frustration with it. That is not a defeat — it is the shape of things. You make peace not by agreeing that the institution is right, but by deciding that your energy is yours to keep.

The problem has not been solved. It has been lived with. You have learned the difference.

Ulmer writes that the egent does not seek resolution but orientation — a way of moving through the apparatus without being entirely consumed by it. This is what you have found: a way to hold the problem without being held by it.

The classroom will be there on Monday. Your students will arrive carrying their own apparatuses, their own compulsory electracies, their own unsolved problems. You will arrive knowing that your work is not to solve those problems but to help the students recognize that the problems are theirs to carry — creatively, generatively, with care.

You are still in it. That is enough.
