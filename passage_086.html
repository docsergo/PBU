Thread 05 — Compulsory Electracy
Future of education — Ulmer's forecast

Like Ulmer, we (fore-)see the future of education as widespread adaptation to an increasingly smart and connected world, including ChatGPT. Electracy provided critical language to describe these changes, this major paradigm shift. Even better, electracy helped us recharacterize our mistakes as experiential texts with vital information. I envision my future pedagogy as a continuation of this present moment: I guide my students through compulsory electracy.

More specifically,

0. My best advice for graduate students in our field and for colleagues on the job market is to know, deeply, in your heart, that it's all luck. If you are successful in obtaining the job you desire, you absolutely deserve it because of your hard work. But you are also incalcuably lucky to have landed a job in a market in which only 2-7 percent of people who enter a doctoral program in the arts and humanities earn a tenure-track position (Caterine 7-8). This statistic renders the term "alt-ac" a misnomer. The "alt" of academia, statistically speaking, is a tenure-track job.
0. Your Text HereNow, on this side of tenure and promotion, I would say to everyone in this field: The job won't love you back.
