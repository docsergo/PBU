Thread 06 — Algorithms & SEO
Surfer content planner

As with our Ahrefs analysis, we started with one primary keyword: "keyword research." Then, we used Surfer's content planner to get the most relevant associated terms that we could add. We next moved these keywords into Surfer's "content editing" module, a feature that crawls the web for the keywords you put in and provides a list of competing pages, each with a "content score." Surfer defines this content score as "a numerical value that ranges from 0 to 100, representing the quality of the content and its relevance to a given keyword" (Czajkowski). The content score factors in usage of relevant keywords and phrases, the title, headings, content length, images, image captions, and more. As the writer adjusts the page to the program's recommendations, the score goes up, and, according to them, the amount of search volume and page traffic should too. The feature also allows the user to choose which pages you would most like to compete with and auto recommends the pages with the best content score.

To see if we could improve the SEO content score of a webpage, we took an article that was ranked further down on the SERP and inserted the text into the content editor. We then chose the most relevent competitors with the highest content score and began updating the text, which was given an initial score of 56 out of 100. We were then provided with a list of keywords which were automatically recommended by an analysis of the competing pages.

With this list of keywords gathered from similar websites, we were able to raise the content score simply by copying and pasting keywords into the text at random, effectively checking the box that the program was looking for. In other words, all we had to do was type a list of keywords, such as "search engine, search volume, keyword research important, search intent, why is keyword, search engine optimization," and the SEO score automatically improved by over twenty percent. Our actions here could be referred to as "keyword stuffing," meaning that the keywords are placed on the page outside of any context and separate from the actual writing. This often occurs in the form of lists or word clouds. Google's search algorithm has been updated to ignore websites that use keyword stuffing, meaning that at this time it is ineffective at reaching either a human or an algorithmic audience. This is important because even with the useful data this program produces, a competent writer is needed to skillfully integrate the keywords into the text.

Whether the content planners and writers are using Google Search Console, Ahrefs, Surfer SEO, or another program to harvest terms, they need to be tactfully included into the text by a writer who understands elements of style. For example, there could be a number of ways in which a writer would incorporate the high-volume phrase "keyword research important." A poor example would be to use the term at the beginning of a sentence and leave out the verb, "is," in order to match the keyword exactly: "Keyword research important because a page cannot rank without relevant keywords." While the search engine may not mind, a reader would likely see this as an error. Grammatical errors such as this could cause readers to become less trusting of the website. A better way to include this term would be to phrase it in a question: "You may be wondering; why is keyword research important?" This allows the keyword to be used correctly and also phrases it into a question, which could also make the term even stronger. Since Surfer SEO recommends that the term be used up to four times, the writer would have to get even more creative with the next three uses; after all, posing it as a question four times on the same page would more than likely stand out negatively to the reader. A skilled writer could use a variety of stylistic tactics to make this work. One strategy would be to combine the end of one sentence with the beginning of another to create the term: "The cornerstone of good SEO starts with keyword research. Important aspects of keyword research include finding the best set of keywords to include on a page." Since the words are still in order, the keyword will still register.
