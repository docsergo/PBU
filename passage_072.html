Thread 01 — Theory
Information is everywhere — third contribution
Also appears in: Algorithms & SEO

The third contribution I retain from Ulmer is that information is everywhere. He writes, "Information is not stored anywhere in particular. Rather, it is stored everywhere. Information is better thought of as 'evoked' than 'found"' (Heruretics 36). We now know that we're actually swimming in information, especially ifwe consider data. Our data is surveilled and mined-extracted currency for the digital economy. Further, Ulmer urged us to switch from "linear inexical" thinking to "network associational" thinking. With actor-network theory and social media "likes" and "dislikes," my students grew up with an acute sense of associative logic. I recognize that we've made that switch because I did not grow up with access to the internet. But this sense of living in informational, associative networks strikes me as prescient and even perhaps foreboding.
